  • Research on Resonance Modes in Photonic Crystal Slabs Based on Effective Medium Approach (undergraduate thesis)

    • Photonic crystal slab is a one-dimensional periodic dielectric structure, which is infinite in the x-y direction and finite in the z direction. It can be used to control the propagation of light, and to experimentally realize the bound state in the continuum (BIC).

        

    This thesis was rated as "Excellent" in my college. By solving the eigenvalue equation of electromagnetic field in isotropic and uniaxial anisotropic media, the exact solution of dynamical matrix is obtained. By applying the 4 × 4 matrix algebra, the transfer matrix of the whole structure is obtained. Then, the matrix equation satisfying the infinite boundary condition is derived, which gives the dispersion relation. Through a carefully designed program, the dispersion of waveguide modes and Fabry–Pérot (F-P) modes in isotropic medium are plotted, and the dispersion of the waveguide mode in uniaxial anisotropic medium are also given. The dispersion under different refractive indexes are compared. The attempting to solve the F-P modes in anisotropic medium is also made.

  • An Extension of the Transfer Matrix Method to Analyzing Acoustic Wave Propagation in Waveguide with Arbitrary Shape

    • This is an article wrote during my undergraduate. It's my very first article, unfortunately, because of its naiveness and my lack of research knowledege, although with great passion, it got several rejection. After failure after failures, I realized objective conditions do matters, and there is still a lot to learn. I wouldn't give up my chance of being a good researcher, I'm not supposed to achieve at one attempt, but through perseverance, there's big chance. Now I'm in USTC, with better reseach resource, knowledge and more capability, I'm truly moving towards the target.
        In this paper, based on 2-D analytical analysis and linear approximation to boundary elements, the transfer matrix method (TMM) is extended to the analysis of the sound transmission in waveguide with arbitrary shape. Based on TMM, the impedance transfer method (ITM) is also developed for calculating the resonance frequency. Moreover, finite element calculations are studied and compared with the new methods. It shows that both methods can provide pretty high accuracy within wide frequency range, and compared to mainstream numerical methods, they require much less computing time.
